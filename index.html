Work/life balance

Self management

Patients can manage their health care outside the hospital or clinic walls and take actions in favour of dealing with disease or chronic illness.

DISEASE COPING SKILLS

Patients develop knowledge of what the disease entails and skills on how to manage its effects in daily life.

Healthy BEHAVIOuRS

Patients become proactive as regards their choice of healthy behaviour that may lead to faster healing or minimising the likelihood of relapse.

Emotional strength

Patients develop strategies to minimise the impact of disease or chronic illness and the emotional challenges they may present so they can lead an emotionally healthier life.

Health literacy

Patients learn the value of taking their prescribed medication as advised, the consequences of not doing so and the benefits of being consistent when taking their prescription.

Responsibility

It is now proven that when a patient becomes an active participant in the decision making process that has to do with themselves, they are more keen on sticking to their health regime.

Family resilience

Relatives of patients are left out of the game when it comes to dealing with unfortunate situations. They, too, can develop resilience and strategies to overcome the impact of disease, support the patient, create a positive environment for themselves and the family member undergoing treatment.
